Boat Donation Stories: Standards for Real Donor Accounts

Understand the consent, verification, privacy, context, and useful detail required before a real donation story is published.

Consent comes first

A responsible story needs publication permission, protected personal information, approved images, and clarity about changed names or details.

Practical facts teach

Condition, ownership, storage, access, paperwork, and decision process are more useful than unexplained praise.

Outcomes are not promises

Acceptance in one place does not predict another. Distance, season, market, marina rules, and documents differ.

Tax claims need restraint

A story should not promote a deduction amount others can expect. Outcomes depend on personal facts and substantiation.

No invented testimonials

Only permissioned, supportable accounts belong here. Hypotheticals must be labeled as scenarios, not represented as donors.

Stories should prepare readers

A useful account shows what records, photos, access facts, uncertainties, and decisions mattered.

Questions to resolve before transfer

  • Who is legally authorized to transfer the boat and trailer?
  • Which title, registration, lien, estate, or documentation records exist?
  • What is the current hull, engine, equipment, and trailer condition?
  • Where is the vessel stored, and what access, fee, or deadline applies?
  • Which acceptance, movement, timing, value, and tax assumptions remain unconfirmed?

Keep the review grounded in evidence

Use current photographs, exact identification numbers, direct facility information, and relevant records. Do not cancel storage, insurance, or security arrangements until ownership has transferred and required notices are complete. We review every boat individually.
