Donate a Jet Ski: PWC and Trailer Checklist

Prepare a personal watercraft donation inquiry with useful ownership, engine, trailer, storage, and access information.

Inventory each unit

For multiple watercraft, list each year, make, model, hull number, registration, key status, and operating condition separately.

Record the trailer

Photograph VIN, bunks, frame, tires, hubs, lights, coupler, and title or registration. Double and modified trailers add questions.

Describe condition

State when it last ran, winterization or flushing, saltwater use, collision damage, missing panels, water intrusion, and whether the engine turns over.

Explain access

Garage, gated community, rack, beach cart, or yard access can involve keys, hours, stairs, sand, equipment, or vendor limits.

Avoid value assumptions

Online asking prices do not establish fair market value. Acknowledgment and tax reporting depend on the completed transaction and rules.

Send complete photos

We review every watercraft individually. Clear records and photos are more useful than a vague non-running description.

Questions to resolve before transfer

  • Who is legally authorized to transfer the boat and trailer?
  • Which title, registration, lien, estate, or documentation records exist?
  • What is the current hull, engine, equipment, and trailer condition?
  • Where is the vessel stored, and what access, fee, or deadline applies?
  • Which acceptance, movement, timing, value, and tax assumptions remain unconfirmed?

Keep the review grounded in evidence

Use current photographs, exact identification numbers, direct facility information, and relevant records. Do not cancel storage, insurance, or security arrangements until ownership has transferred and required notices are complete. We review every boat individually.
