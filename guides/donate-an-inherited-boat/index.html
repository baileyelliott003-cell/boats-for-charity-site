Donate an Inherited Boat: Estate and Ownership Checklist

Learn what executors, heirs, trustees, and families should gather before donating a boat from an estate.

Confirm legal authority

Possession is not always authority. Estate letters, trust certification, a small-estate process, court approval, or multiple heirs may be involved.

Find ownership records

Locate title, registration, documentation, lien release, bill of sale, insurance, marina contract, trailer papers, and owner names.

Protect the property

Keep the boat secure, manage bilge pumps and weather exposure, maintain required coverage, and communicate with storage providers.

Document condition

Use current photographs and a factual last-operating date. Note keys, removed equipment, storm damage, and personal property.

Resolve claims

Loans, storage balances, taxes, mechanic liens, surviving co-owners, or company ownership can prevent a clean transfer.

Share records carefully

Provide requested authority documents and redact unrelated sensitive information. A death certificate alone may not prove transfer authority.

Questions to resolve before transfer

  • Who is legally authorized to transfer the boat and trailer?
  • Which title, registration, lien, estate, or documentation records exist?
  • What is the current hull, engine, equipment, and trailer condition?
  • Where is the vessel stored, and what access, fee, or deadline applies?
  • Which acceptance, movement, timing, value, and tax assumptions remain unconfirmed?

Keep the review grounded in evidence

Use current photographs, exact identification numbers, direct facility information, and relevant records. Do not cancel storage, insurance, or security arrangements until ownership has transferred and required notices are complete. We review every boat individually.
