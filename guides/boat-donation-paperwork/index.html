Boat Donation Paperwork: Complete Document Checklist

Organize boat, trailer, lien, estate, marina, transfer, acknowledgment, and tax records.

Boat identity

Gather title, registration, federal documentation if applicable, hull-number photo, official number, builder plate, and prior bill of sale.

Owner authority

Match the printed owner. Co-owners, companies, trusts, estates, powers of attorney, divorces, and guardianships may require support.

Liens and balances

Locate lender releases, mechanic-lien notices, marina balances, storage invoices, tax records, and impound notices.

Trailer record

Capture trailer VIN, title or registration, plate, owner, weight rating, and condition. The hull record does not transfer the trailer.

Transfer file

Keep signed assignments, bills of sale, receipts, key inventories, marina notices, insurance communication, and liability filings.

Tax file

Retain acknowledgment, disposition notice if issued, appraisal, Form 8283 materials, photos, basis records, and professional advice.

Questions to resolve before transfer

  • Who is legally authorized to transfer the boat and trailer?
  • Which title, registration, lien, estate, or documentation records exist?
  • What is the current hull, engine, equipment, and trailer condition?
  • Where is the vessel stored, and what access, fee, or deadline applies?
  • Which acceptance, movement, timing, value, and tax assumptions remain unconfirmed?

Keep the review grounded in evidence

Use current photographs, exact identification numbers, direct facility information, and relevant records. Do not cancel storage, insurance, or security arrangements until ownership has transferred and required notices are complete. We review every boat individually.
