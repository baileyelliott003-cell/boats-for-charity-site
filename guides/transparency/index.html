Transparency: How Boats for Charity Reviews Boat Donations

See principles covering review limits, ownership, transportation uncertainty, tax boundaries, disclosures, and donor records.

Facts decide the review

Age, type, or city alone does not decide feasibility. Condition, ownership, storage, access, location, trailer, liens, and disposition matter together.

Inquiry is not acceptance

A submission begins review. It does not transfer ownership or guarantee acceptance, pickup, transport, timing, proceeds, or tax treatment.

Transfer must be clear

Owners should keep the vessel secure and maintain obligations until transfer is complete. Documents should identify property and signer.

Problems belong up front

Damage, missing engines, sinking, contamination, liens, fees, inaccessible sites, missing records, and estate issues should be disclosed.

Tax communication has limits

Required transaction documents can be provided, but Boats for Charity cannot promise a deduction or determine a donor’s tax position.

Corrections matter

Report a possible page or document error with its location and supporting facts so it can be reviewed.

Questions to resolve before transfer

  • Who is legally authorized to transfer the boat and trailer?
  • Which title, registration, lien, estate, or documentation records exist?
  • What is the current hull, engine, equipment, and trailer condition?
  • Where is the vessel stored, and what access, fee, or deadline applies?
  • Which acceptance, movement, timing, value, and tax assumptions remain unconfirmed?

Keep the review grounded in evidence

Use current photographs, exact identification numbers, direct facility information, and relevant records. Do not cancel storage, insurance, or security arrangements until ownership has transferred and required notices are complete. We review every boat individually.
